# Emergency Procedures at Amdocs Offices

## Purpose

This document outlines emergency response procedures for Amdocs office locations, including fire, medical emergencies, natural disasters, security threats, and evacuation protocols. All employees must familiarize themselves with these procedures for their primary and frequently visited office locations.

## Scope

This procedure applies to all Amdocs employees, contractors, visitors, and vendors present on Amdocs premises during an emergency event. Site-specific variations (e.g., earthquake procedures in Israel, tornado procedures in St. Louis) are posted on each floor's emergency action plan board and on the intranet site page.

## Procedure

1. **Know your emergency exits and assembly points.** Upon starting at an office or visiting a new floor, locate the nearest emergency exit, fire extinguisher, AED (Automated External Defibrillator), and floor assembly point. Maps are posted near elevators and break rooms. Assembly points are listed on `https://emergency.amdocs.internal`.

2. **Fire emergency.**
   - Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station if you discover fire or smoke
   - Call the local emergency number (Israel: 102, US: 911, India: 101) and internal security ext. 4910
   - Evacuate immediately via stairs — do not use elevators
   - Proceed to your floor's designated assembly point
   - Do not re-enter the building until the all-clear is announced by Floor Wardens or emergency services

3. **Medical emergency.**
   - Call local emergency services and internal security ext. 4910
   - Do not move the injured person unless in immediate danger
   - Trained First Aid Responders (identified by green vest in emergency kits) will assist
   - AED locations are marked with red signage on every floor

4. **Security threat (active threat, suspicious package, bomb threat).**
   - **Run:** Evacuate if a safe path exists
   - **Hide:** If evacuation is not possible, lock/barricade doors, silence phones, stay out of sight
   - **Fight:** As last resort, act decisively to disrupt the threat
   - Call security ext. 4910 or local police. Do not pull fire alarm unless fire is confirmed (may evacuate into danger)

5. **Earthquake (Israel, applicable regions).**
   - Drop, Cover, Hold On under a sturdy desk
   - After shaking stops, evacuate via stairs to assembly point
   - Avoid elevators for 30 minutes after shaking

6. **Register for Amdocs Alert System.** Ensure your mobile number is registered at `https://emergency.amdocs.internal` to receive SMS and push notifications during emergencies. Update when changing phone numbers.

7. **Report to Floor Warden at assembly point.** Floor Wardens (identified by orange vests) conduct headcounts. Report missing colleagues to the Warden — do not re-enter to search.

8. **Post-emergency.** Follow instructions from Emergency Response Team. Incident debriefs are conducted within 72 hours. Submit observations via the **Post-Incident Report** form on the emergency portal.

## Important Notes

- **Floor Warden program:** Each floor has 2–4 trained Floor Wardens. Volunteer by contacting `emergency@amdocs.com`.
- Emergency drills are conducted quarterly. Participation is mandatory; continued work during drills is prohibited.
- Visitors and contractors are the host employee's responsibility during evacuations.
- Business continuity: during extended office closures, activate your team's BCP plan (SharePoint **BCP > Team Plans**). Remote work is the default continuity mode.
