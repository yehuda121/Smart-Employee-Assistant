# Clean Desk Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes Amdocs requirements for maintaining clean and secure workspaces to protect sensitive information from unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure. The Clean Desk Policy applies to all work environments including offices, home offices, and shared/co-working spaces.

## Scope

This policy applies to all Amdocs employees, contractors, and visitors during and outside business hours. It covers physical documents, storage media, devices, authentication tokens, and visual exposure of sensitive information on screens or whiteboards.

## Procedure

1. **Clear desks at end of day.** Before leaving your workspace each day (or when away for more than 30 minutes), secure the following:
   - Lock all Confidential and Restricted documents in desk drawers or filing cabinets
   - Remove papers from printers and shredder input trays
   - Store portable media (USB drives, external hard drives) in locked drawers
   - Clear whiteboards of Confidential or Restricted content (photograph if needed, then erase)
   - Eject and store CD/DVD media

2. **Secure authentication factors.** Badge, YubiKey, RSA token, and corporate credit card must be on your person or locked away — never left on the desk. Laptops must be locked to desk cables in open office areas or stored in locked drawers overnight.

3. **Lock computer screens.** Configure automatic screen lock after 5 minutes of inactivity (enforced via MDM policy on corporate devices). Manually lock (Windows: Win+L; Mac: Ctrl+Cmd+Q) when stepping away. Do not disable screen lock.

4. **Manage visitor visibility.** When visitors or unauthorized personnel are present in your work area, close Confidential documents, minimize sensitive screens, and avoid discussing Restricted information audibly.

5. **Home office requirements.** Remote employees must maintain a dedicated workspace with:
   - Door or visual barrier preventing household member access to work materials
   - Locked storage for printed Confidential/Restricted documents
   - Privacy screen on laptop if working in public spaces
   - No Amdocs whiteboards or posted documents visible from windows or video call backgrounds

6. **Clean desk audits.** Information Security conducts random Clean Desk audits quarterly at office locations. Findings are reported to department managers. Three consecutive failures trigger mandatory **SEC-120: Physical Security Awareness** training.

7. **Report unattended sensitive materials.** If you find Confidential or Restricted documents left in common areas, printers, or conference rooms, deliver them to Corporate Security (ext. 4910) or the document owner — do not read or distribute the contents.

## Important Notes

- Clean Desk applies to hot-desking environments: clear the desk completely at end of day since another employee will use it the next day.
- Personal items (photos, mugs, plants) are permitted but must not obstruct secure storage or display sensitive information.
- Conference rooms: erase whiteboards and remove printed materials after every meeting. Uncleared whiteboards are the most common audit finding.
- Shred bins (blue, cross-cut) are located on every floor. Do not use regular trash for Confidential or Restricted documents.
- Locking desk drawers: keys are issued by Facilities upon desk assignment. Lost keys must be reported to Facilities for lock replacement.
