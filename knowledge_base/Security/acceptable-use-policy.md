# Acceptable Use Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines acceptable and prohibited uses of Amdocs information technology resources, including computers, networks, email, internet access, and cloud services. The Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) protects Amdocs, its employees, customers, and partners from legal, security, and reputational risks.

## Scope

This policy applies to all users of Amdocs IT resources: employees, contractors, temporary staff, and authorized third parties. It covers all devices (corporate-managed and BYOD accessing Amdocs data), networks (office, VPN, cloud), and services (email, internet, SaaS applications).

## Procedure

1. **Permitted use.** Amdocs IT resources may be used for:
   - Business activities directly related to your Amdocs role
   - Limited personal use that does not interfere with work, consume significant resources, or violate other policies (e.g., brief personal email, checking personal bank account during lunch)
   - Professional development aligned with your role and IDP

2. **Prohibited use.** The following activities are strictly forbidden:
   - Accessing, storing, or distributing illegal content
   - Installing unauthorized software or hardware (KB-IT-005)
   - Sharing credentials or circumventing authentication controls
   - Using Amdocs resources for personal commercial activities or secondary employment
   - Sending harassing, discriminatory, or threatening communications
   - Mining cryptocurrency on Amdocs devices or networks
   - Connecting unauthorized devices to Amdocs networks (personal routers, NAS devices)
   - Exfiltrating Amdocs data to personal cloud accounts (Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud)
   - Attempting to access systems or data beyond your authorized level
   - Disabling or tampering with security controls (antivirus, MDM, DLP, encryption)

3. **Email and communication standards.** Amdocs email must not be used for: chain letters, political campaigning, unsolicited bulk messages, or content that could damage Amdocs reputation. All communications are subject to monitoring and retention (7 years).

4. **Internet usage.** Amdocs provides unrestricted internet access for business purposes. Personal browsing is permitted within reasonable limits. Blocked categories (enforced by Zscaler web proxy): malware sites, known phishing domains, illegal content, and high-bandwidth streaming during business hours.

5. **Social media.** Employees may identify their Amdocs affiliation on personal social media but must include a disclaimer ("Views are my own"). Do not share Confidential or Restricted Amdocs information on social media. Official Amdocs social media posts require Marketing approval.

6. **Software and intellectual property.** Software installed on Amdocs devices must be properly licensed (KB-IT-015). Open-source software used in Amdocs products must be registered in the Open Source Registry. Do not install pirated or cracked software.

7. **Report violations.** Suspected AUP violations by others should be reported to security@amdocs.com or your manager. Whistleblower protections apply to good-faith reports.

8. **Acknowledge the policy.** All employees acknowledge the AUP annually in Workday during compliance training recertification (**COMP-100** module).

## Important Notes

- Amdocs reserves the right to monitor, log, and audit all activity on corporate IT resources without prior notice, consistent with local privacy laws.
- Personal use privilege may be revoked for any employee under investigation or with a history of policy violations.
- Contractors are subject to the same AUP. Vendor managers are responsible for communicating requirements.
- Violations may result in: warning, access suspension, termination, and/or legal action depending on severity.
- The AUP supplements but does not replace regional policies (GDPR, CCPA, Israel Privacy Protection Law) and employment agreements.
