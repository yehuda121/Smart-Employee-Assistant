# Hardware Replacement and Repair

## Purpose

This document outlines the procedure for requesting replacement or repair of Amdocs corporate hardware including laptops, monitors, docking stations, keyboards, and peripherals. Timely hardware replacement maintains employee productivity and ensures devices remain compliant with security standards.

## Scope

This procedure applies to all Amdocs-issued hardware tracked in the IT Asset Management system (ServiceNow CMDB). It covers malfunctioning devices, accidental damage, normal wear-and-tear replacements, and warranty-related repairs. New laptop requests for first-time assignments follow KB-IT-004.

## Procedure

1. **Diagnose the issue.** Before requesting replacement, attempt basic troubleshooting: restart the device, check for OS updates, and test with an alternate peripheral if applicable. Consult the **Amdocs IT Knowledge Base** for common fixes.

2. **Determine replacement category.** Select the appropriate ServiceNow form:
   - **Hardware > Laptop Repair/Replacement** — laptops with performance, battery, or physical damage issues
   - **Hardware > Peripheral Replacement** — monitors, docks, keyboards, mice, headsets
   - **Hardware > Emergency Replacement** — device completely non-functional and blocking all work

3. **Submit the request.** Include your Employee ID, device asset tag (format: ADM-IT-XXXXXX), description of the issue, troubleshooting steps attempted, and whether you need a temporary loaner device.

4. **Obtain manager approval.** Standard replacements require manager approval. Emergency replacements are auto-approved for P1 business impact with post-hoc manager notification.

5. **Schedule device evaluation.** IT Endpoint Engineering may require an in-person or remote diagnostic session. For Ra'anana, St. Louis, and Pune offices, visit the IT Walk-Up Center (hours: 09:00–17:00 local time). Remote employees receive a prepaid shipping label for mail-in evaluation.

6. **Receive replacement or repair.** Warranty-covered repairs are completed within 3–5 business days. Out-of-warranty devices are replaced if repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement value. Loaner laptops (Dell Latitude 5440) are available for the duration of repair.

7. **Return defective hardware.** Return the original device within 5 business days of receiving the replacement. Failure to return may result in a chargeback equal to the device's depreciated value. Data destruction certificates are issued upon request for devices containing Restricted data.

## Important Notes

- Accidental damage (drops, liquid spills) is covered once per device lifecycle. Subsequent damage incidents require department cost center chargeback at full replacement cost.
- Battery replacement is offered when capacity falls below 80% on devices older than 18 months.
- Monitors are refreshed on a 5-year cycle. Early replacement requires justification (dead pixels, flickering, resolution inadequacy for role).
- Do not dispose of Amdocs hardware personally. All devices must be returned to IT for secure data wiping and environmentally compliant recycling (Amdocs is e-Stewards certified).
- Asset tag must remain affixed to the device. Removing or tampering with asset tags violates IT policy.
